# v0.9.0 Plan

Purpose: promote the first surface-temperature result from mapped output to a
tolerance-gated conformance case.

## Promoted Case

`surface_temperature_nomass_001` is the only v0.9 surface-temperature
conformance case.

It covers:

- one `Zone`
- six opaque `BuildingSurface:Detailed` surfaces
- `Material:NoMass`
- one `Construction`
- adiabatic outside boundary conditions
- no internal gains, windows, solar, infiltration, HVAC, plant, moisture, EMS,
  or sizing-period claim
- hourly `Zone Mean Air Temperature`
- hourly `Surface Inside Face Temperature` for `FLOOR`
- hourly `Surface Outside Face Temperature` for `FLOOR`

## Source Reference

The runtime initialization remains aligned with EnergyPlus 26.1.0:

- `src/EnergyPlus/DataHeatBalance.hh`
- `DataHeatBalance::ZoneInitialTemp`
- `DataHeatBalance::SurfInitialTemp`
- `src/EnergyPlus/HeatBalanceSurfaceManager.cc`

For v0.9, Rust writes the declared surface face temperatures into `ResultStore`
for the no-mass adiabatic equilibrium case. This is a surface-state report for
the declared output variables only.

## Tolerance Gate

The case manifest declares:

- `comparison_class = "conformance"`
- `conformance_claim = true`
- output variables:
  - `Zone Mean Air Temperature`
  - `Surface Inside Face Temperature`
  - `Surface Outside Face Temperature`
- frequency: hourly
- source: ESO
- tolerance: `max_abs = 0.000001`, `max_rmse = 0.000001` for `zone-state`
- tolerance: `max_abs = 0.000001`, `max_rmse = 0.000001` for `surface-state`
- blocking gate: `scripts/dev.cmd compare-surface-temperature-conformance`

The gate writes:

- `.runtime/surface-temperature-conformance/26.1.0/surface_temperature_nomass_001/compare/compare-summary.json`
- `.runtime/surface-temperature-conformance/26.1.0/surface_temperature_nomass_001/compare/compare-report.md`

## Not Claimed

This is not a fenestration or solar-radiation claim. It is also not a dynamic
exterior heat-balance claim.

Not claimed in v0.9:

- windows, glazing heat balance, or transmitted solar radiation
- inside or outside solar radiation heat-gain parity
- longwave/shortwave radiation exchange parity
- wind, ground, sky, or detailed exterior convection parity
- surface conduction heat transfer rate conformance
- mass construction transient response
- infiltration, ventilation, HVAC, plant, or thermostat behavior
- warmup convergence parity beyond the declared initial temperature default
