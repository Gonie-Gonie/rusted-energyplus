# v0.5.0 Plan

Purpose: static geometry, construction, and internal-gain evidence for input
interpretation, without claiming heat-balance or HVAC numerical conformance.

Evidence targets:

- EIO `Zone Information` comparison for zone surface count, floor area,
  volume, and exterior gross wall area
- EIO `HeatTransfer Surface` comparison for surface class, net/gross area,
  azimuth, and tilt
- EIO `Construction CTF` and `Material CTF Summary` comparison for the first
  construction/material thermal-input subset
- EIO `OtherEquipment Internal Gains Nominal` comparison for nominal internal
  gains and convective fraction
- ESO `Zone Total Internal Convective Heating Rate` trace comparison for the
  first internal-gain runtime series
- `surface_geometry_001`, `construction_materials_001`, and
  `internal_gains_001` case manifests using explicit static or hourly output
  requests

This milestone is about input/static evidence and nominal internal-gain
plumbing. It does not claim surface heat-transfer, zone air temperature,
fenestration, shading, coordinate-system variants, HVAC, plant conformance, or
a tolerance-gated conformance release until reports and blocking gates are
promoted accordingly.

Locked v0.5 variable surface:

- zone geometry: surface count, floor area, volume, and exterior gross wall
  area from EIO `Zone Information`
- surface geometry: class, net area, gross area, azimuth, and tilt from EIO
  `HeatTransfer Surface`
- construction/material: construction count, outside material identity, layer
  count, construction thermal conductance, material thickness, conductivity,
  density, specific heat, and thermal resistance from EIO `Construction CTF`
  and `Material CTF Summary`
- internal gains: zone floor area, equipment level, equipment per floor area,
  latent/radiant/lost/convected fractions from EIO
  `OtherEquipment Internal Gains Nominal`, plus the first hourly
  `Zone Total Internal Convective Heating Rate` ESO trace

EIO parser boundary:

- EIO rows are trusted only as EnergyPlus oracle extraction artifacts.
- Matching these rows means the current Rust typed input interpretation agrees
  with selected EnergyPlus static summaries for the dedicated fixtures.
- EIO parser success is not a claim that EnergyPlus CTF, surface heat balance,
  solar distribution, or zone air heat balance algorithms have been ported.
